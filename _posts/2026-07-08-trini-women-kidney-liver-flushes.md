Ask a Trinidadian grandmother about "cleaning out" the kidneys or liver and she'll likely have an opinion, and probably a recipe. Kidney and liver flushes are a long-standing part of Caribbean herbal tradition, and they're still a routine many Trini women return to a few times a year. Here's what the tradition actually involves.

## What is a kidney or liver flush, traditionally?

A kidney or liver flush is a herbal wellness routine, typically involving a herbal preparation taken over a set number of days. In Caribbean tradition, flushes have long been treated as a periodic "reset" routine, often timed around a change of season or simply when someone feels it's time for one. This is a traditional wellness practice, not a medical treatment, and it isn't intended to diagnose, cure, treat, or prevent any disease.

## Why do Trini women keep this tradition going?

For many families, it's less about a specific outcome and more about routine and ritual, the same way some households have a set day for a herbal tea or a particular Sunday cook-up. It's passed down as something "you just do" a few times a year, often alongside other wellness habits like drinking more water, eating lighter, or cutting back on processed food for a stretch.
